1.3 The Internet has grown into a ubiquitous infrastructure that societies all over the world are increasingly reliant on. To which extent and how does the internet have political consequences? Note down some examples and discuss with your colleagues. (From module 1.3)

The mobile phones with internet access gives us the possibility to gain information at any given time. The constant access to information can be overwhelming and can distract from the tasks humans have in their everyday life. The attention span has dropped for humans since the introduction of smartphones. Even though we now can be more informed and always check the news, we do not have the attention span to read the news in detail. The fight for our attention creates exaggerated headlines that do not necessarily correspond with reality, which makes us question our priorities, what is important and what is worth my time? The internet has political consequences in the form of echo chambers.

Companies use your personal data to show you the information that you like and unconsciously want to hear. In that way, you will for example get political messages in your social media feed from politicians who think just like you. There are also a vast variety of forums where like minded people find support in their views. This is a dangerous development where political views do not get challenged. The less time spent challenging our own views, the less tolerant we get for other views than our own. It is difficult to state if we are less tolerant now than before since the variety of opinions we now see in forums were less exposed.

1.4 Consider the statement "The person who invented the train invented the train crash." What are your thoughts about the societal responsibility of innovators? (From module 1.4)

To state that innovators cause accidents as a result of their innovations can in some cases be true and in some not. If we look at possibly one of the biggest controversies in Oslo now, the electrical scooters are often blamed for the accidents they are in, and not the persons riding them. In most of the cases, alcohol, bad judgment, possibilities for high speed in crowded areas or lack of experience or control are the biggest reasons for the accidents (Trygg Trafikk, u.å.). Should the inventor of the electrical scooters be blamed for this, the politicians who made the laws, or the people that are involved in the accidents.

Implementation of new technology needs regulation of usage to not cause negative consequences. The users of new technology also have to test the products to find out how to use them correctly. Innovators have a responsibility to a certain extent, but if you look at the bigger picture then nothing new would ever have been invented if they were to take full responsibility for every possible outcome of using the invented item or technology. Could we blame the inventor of paper for all paper cuts? Although an inventor in a way also invents opportunities for accidents, it is up to the people to implement the idea in a safe way.
